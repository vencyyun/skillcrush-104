Confusion happens when you have an overproductive garden! What to do with Mushrooms?

Mushrooms are excellent served with japanese eggplant. These long legged beauties in their purple skins are delicately tender and pale when cut open.

Harvest them in summer, when they are the most productive, often each plant produces 4 kilos, long and thin, they spring from the node of it's purple flower.

I love a splash of soy sauce, maple syrup, seasame oil, fried up with some fresh shitaki mushrooms. I salivate thinking about them.

The best eggplant companion

Serve it all on a bed of brown whole grain rice

Don't forget to dunk your eggplant in more dipping sauce
